The Legend

What are the Backrooms?

According to the internet legend, reality is like a video game. Sometimes, there is a "glitch" in the code. If you step in the wrong place, you can accidentally "noclip" (fall through the floor or walls) out of the real world.

You don't die. Instead, you wake up in The Backrooms.

It is an endless maze of empty office rooms. There is nothing but the stink of old, moist carpets, yellow wallpaper, and the maddening sound of broken fluorescent lights.

Modals of Deduction

We use these specific modal verbs to express how certain we are about something.

MUST
You are 99% sure it is true.
"There are no humans here. That shadow must be a monster."
MIGHT / MAY
You are 50% sure. It's a possibility.
"Don't drink that water. It might be poisonous."
CAN't
You are 99% sure it is IMPOSSIBLE.
"That can't be the exit. We just walked past this wall!"

Survival Rules

The First Conditional

In survival situations, we use the First Conditional to talk about a real possibility in the future.

If + Present Simple , Will + Verb

"If you go into the dark, you will need a flashlight."

Complete the survival tips for the Backrooms:

If you hear a monster, you will... If the lights go out, I will...

The Encounter

You keep walking. Suddenly, you freeze. About 50 meters in front of you, a tall, black shadow steps into the hallway. It has no face. It turns toward you.

It must be an entity. It screams and starts running at you incredibly fast.

Make your choice quickly!

The Second Conditional

What if the situation is imaginary or hypothetical? We use the Second Conditional.

If + Past Simple , Would + Verb

"If I saw an entity, I would run."

Hypothetical Survival:

If I had a weapon, I would... If my friend fell in the Backrooms, I would...
